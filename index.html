The Evolving Social Engineering Threat

Social engineering exploits human trust to bypass technical security controls. The rise of AI-powered attacks has made these threats more sophisticated and harder to detect. This section details the common attack vectors and highlights specific vulnerabilities within key Australian industries.

Phishing & BEC

Deceptive emails or messages from seemingly trusted sources to steal credentials or initiate fraudulent transactions. Business Email Compromise (BEC) is a highly damaging variant.

Baiting

Luring victims with tempting offers (e.g., free software, infected USB drives) to install malware or gain system access.

Pretexting

Creating a fabricated scenario or "pretext" to manipulate a target into divulging information or performing an action.

AI-Powered Attacks

Using AI for voice cloning and deepfakes to create highly convincing impersonations, making detection extremely difficult.
